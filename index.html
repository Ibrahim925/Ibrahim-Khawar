I'm a 17-year-old 11th grader at Hillfield Strathallan College, born and raised in Toronto. I solve problems with technology.


When I was 13, I taught myself Python. I immediately got hooked to coding and technology in general because I saw it as one of the best ways to solve the world's biggest problems. I continued teaching myself how to code, expanding to languages like JavaScript, C++, and Java.


I eventually ventured beyond coding, learning and building with emerging technologies like artificial intelligence and brain-computer interfaces. I read numerous research papers, watched MIT OCW lectures, and met with researchers to learn about how these technologies are helping humanity. One of my research projects used AI and big data to explore ways to mitigate urban heat islands and was published by Canadian Science Publishing.


I continued building up my skills and eventually landed an internship at LogiSense. I got experience working on critical enterprise software with an incredible team of innovators and pitched ideas to executives at the company.


After my internship, I founded a tech startup called Tablingos. The core product is a SaaS through which users can create data pipelines that are capable of validating, transforming, and exporting data, all without writing a single line of code. I created this for two reasons (one long-term, one short-term):


  1. Data is at the core of virtually every emerging technology that is going to improve how we humans live our lives. However, this data is rarely clean and usable as-is. To enable these innovative technologies, I am offering a way to clean, augment, and transport data securely.

  2. Businesses today face data quality issues that are costly to resolve and delay project timelines, for example, during customer onboarding. Existing solutions are expensive, unintutive, and require high engineering efforts. Tablingos solves this by allowing teams to validate, transform, and migrate enterprise data with zero engineering requirements and far shorter timelines.
