Pocket Prosthetic and Remote Care

Pocket Prosthetic came out of my time at Holland Bloorview and a simple question: how can prosthetic care become more accessible for patients who cannot repeatedly travel to clinics for fittings?

The app uses an iPhone's LiDAR sensor to capture a residual limb scan and send that information directly to a prosthetist, alongside the supporting context needed for review. The goal was to reduce friction without compromising the quality of care.

It was one of the winning projects at Hack the 6ix, but more importantly, it showed me how much thoughtful product work can matter in healthcare.
